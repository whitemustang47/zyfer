Project Overview:

Project Phases:

Planning and Design:

Recognized the growing interest in gesture-controlled devices and identified an opportunity to develop a gesture-controlled RC car.
Collaboratively brainstormed the project requirements and functionalities to ensure the successful implementation of a gesture-controlled RC car.
Allocated tasks among team members based on their individual skills and expertise, fostering efficient collaboration throughout the project.

Hardware Assembly:

Acquired essential components, including aluminium rods, H-bridge, servo motors for steering, and 2 DC motors for engine, to lay the foundation for the RC car.
Meticulously constructed the car base using aluminium rods, ensuring a sturdy and durable framework for the subsequent assembly.
Seamlessly integrated the H-bridge for motor control and meticulously attached servo motors for steering and DC motors for propulsion, ensuring precise control and smooth operation of the RC car.

Gesture Recognition Model Development:

Gathered a diverse dataset for gesture recognition, encompassing a wide range of hand movements and gestures.
Leveraged the powerful Edge Impulse platform to train a robust gesture recognition model, capable of accurately identifying and interpreting various hand gestures.
Fine-tuned and optimized the model for embedded systems, ensuring efficient performance and low-power consumption on the ESP-EYE board.

Integration with ESP-EYE:

Successfully embedded the trained gesture recognition model onto the ESP-EYE board, leveraging its capabilities for real-time gesture detection.
Configured the ESP-EYE board to seamlessly capture and process hand gestures in real-time, laying the foundation for intuitive control of the RC car.

Communication Setup:

Established a reliable communication channel between the ESP-EYE and ESP32 on the RC car, leveraging the ESP-NOW protocol for seamless communication.
Implemented robust protocols for transmitting gesture commands from the ESP-EYE board to the RC car, ensuring accurate and responsive control in various operating conditions.

Testing and Debugging:

Conducted rigorous testing of the entire system to validate functionality and reliability under real-world conditions.
Identified and addressed hardware and software issues promptly, ensuring the smooth operation of the gesture-controlled RC car.
